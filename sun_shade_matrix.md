**Legend:**
- **Yes** = Tolerates this light level
- **Best** = Optimal / preferred light level
- **No** = Does not tolerate this light level
- **Caution** = Can handle it but with caveats (extra water, some burn risk, etc.)
- **--** = Not specifically discussed

## Quick Reference: Best Plants by Light Condition

### Full Sun Champions
- Coconut Palm, Bismarck Palm, Royal Palm
- Johannis Bromeliad, Margarita Bromeliad
- Pinocchio Croton
- Louisiana Red Copper Leaf, Blaze Copper Leaf
- Bougainvillea, Plumeria, Desert Rose
- Lantana, Dwarf Ixora, Blue My Mind, Wedelia
- Yucca Rostrata, Americana Agave
- Japanese Blueberry

### Filtered Light Favorites (Designer's Sweet Spot)
- Brazilian Red Cloak (preferred)
- Sanchezia, Java White Copper Leaf
- Petra Croton, Iceton Croton, Magnificent Croton
- Red Cone Ginger, Variegated Tapioca
- Cat Palm, Areca Palm
- Rojo Congo Philodendron
- Monstera (morning sun + filtered afternoon)
- Psycho Coffee, Foxtail Fern

### Full Shade Stars
- Lady Palm (Rhapis), Cat Palm, Licuala Palm
- Silver Bay Aglaonema (handles deep shade with no light)
- Xanadu Philodendron, Burle Marx Philodendron
- Bird's Nest Fern
- Farfugium (Leopard Plant)
- Song of India (versatile through all light levels)
- Ground Orchid
- Dwarf Pentas (blooms even in shade)
- Imperialis Bromeliad (full sun to full shade)
- Sanchezia (full shade with good color)
- Heliconia

---

*Note: All light tolerance data is specific to Zone 10A (Vero Beach, FL) conditions. Plants in more northern zones may have different sun/shade requirements. "Afternoon sun" in South Florida is significantly more intense than in temperate climates. Many plants described as "needing protection" specifically need protection from harsh late afternoon (2:00-5:00 PM) summer rays.*
